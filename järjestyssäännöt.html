In English

Possibility to appear in pictures

In the convention there is many photographers so coming to the event means you accept the possibility that you might be seen in pictures or videos.

Intentional person or group pictures do always need consent.

Props

At the Convention, real guns/weapons, even deactivated ones and deceptively similar imitations are not allowed. From non-hazardous materials like plastic and cardboard made weapon props are allowed!

Airsoft guns arent allowed. With the Sword props you cannot violently move them around or play fighting in the convention area.

All gun props need to be showed when entering so the security guards can check them.

Intoxication

No intoxicants may be brought to the event. Persons found in an intoxicated state will be removed from the event and the police will be called if necessary. There is no smoking area in kampushalli or its courtyard. If you plan to smoke, do so in the designated smoking areas a little further away from the convention grounds. E-cigarettes are not allowed inside.

Theft

No theft or pickpocketing of any kind is allowed at the event. All thefts will be reported to the police immediately.

Outfits and dressing

If you dress in costume, remember that the convention is a public space. Videos and photos will be taken of the event, so dress in a fitting to show up on social media or in a magazine.

If you are unsure about if your outfit is appropriate, please contact the organizers via Instagram, email or our website.

Koacon reserves the right to intervene in outfits deemed offensive or inappropriate.

Harassment and Bullying

Koacon does not tolerate any form of harassment or bullying. Disruptive individuals will be removed from the event. There will be security guards and a harassment contact person present at the convention, who you can approach during the event. You can identify the harassment contact person by their pink high-visibility vest.

Usual good manners

Put your trash in the trash cans and bottles and cans in the designated places. Do not block hallways or stairs. Remember to respect the personal space of other visitors. Be respectful of others in the space.

KoaCon's Principles for a safer space:

Respect the physical and psychological state of others, their right to self-determination, and do not touch anyone without permission.

Do not mock, ridicule, belittle or embarrass anyone through your words, behaviour or actions. Refrain from criticising appearance, gossiping or perpetuating stereotypes.

Don't make assumptions based on appearance or behavior. Don't make assumptions about someone's sexuality, gender, nationality, ethnicity, religion, values, socioeconomic background, health, or ability to function.

Give space. Try to ensure that everyone has a chance to participate in discussions. Don't push others' opinions and give them a chance to speak. Respect the privacy of others and handle sensitive topics respectfully.

Listen and learn. Accept new topics, people, and perspectives with an open mind.

Apologize if you have intentionally or unintentionally offended others.
